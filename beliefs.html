    • Beliefs are what people of a tradition accept and have faith in that dictates their choices and actions which can be founded by a narrative or event. It guides people towards a particular mindset or actions. What people believe is the reason behind why they do certain things and what affect it has on their lives.

    Catholics

    The Catholic tradition believes that the ritual of baptism is essential for a person's salvation. They also believe that being baptised gives you special graces in your life which is why they perform baptism to babies. Catholics believe there is a spiritual change in your sould once you are baptised so you cannot be baptised more than once.

    Baptism is performed in the catholic tradition to cleanse someone of their original sin which is sin you are born with. The Catholic tradition has the belief that once you are baptised you have entered the life of Christ and the Catholic Church but this change must be honoured and carried.

    Protestants

    The Protestant tradition believes that it is a person's own choice when they want to be baptised.

    Protestants believe that baptism is just an outward symbolic act that signifies how they have repented and God has cleansed their sin.
