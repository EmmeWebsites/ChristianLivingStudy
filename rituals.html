    • A ritual is a fixed practice of a way somthing is done that has often been passed down through generations. As a form of worship, rituals hold a spiritual meaning which people show they are encorporatig into their lives when they participate in the ritual.

    Catholics

    The Catholic ritual of baptism is often done to babies to cleanse them of their original sin. Parents consent for their child's baptsim. A priest pours water over the baby's head three times representing the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit who's power washes the original sin. Chrism oil is then used to anoint the baby’s head in the shape of a cross by the baby’s parents and God parents to signify how the child has been filled with the Holy spirit. A white garment is then given to the baby to represent how they have been cleansed of their original sin. Adults can also participate in baptism. A Priest or Deacon must perform a Catholic baptism.

    Protestants

    In the protestant ritual of baptism a person is fully submerged in the water. A person doesn't participate in the ritual of Baptism until they are a teenager or adult as protestants believe that a person should make the choice themselves to be baptised.

    As the person enters the water it signifies them putting there old self behind them. As they are under the water it shows that God has cleaned them from there sin and as they emerge from the water they have committed to following a life Christ. A baptism can be performed by a pastor or a significant person in the baptised person's walk with God.
